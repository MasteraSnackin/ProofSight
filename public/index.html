Local-first inspection appliance

AI health & safety inspections, running on a Raspberry Pi.

ProofSight captures webcam evidence, rejects bad images, analyses visible hazards, writes inspection reports, logs traces, and keeps a human in the approval loop — without turning the workplace camera into a cloud dependency.

safety behaviour

Bad evidence is refused

Dark, blank, tiny or obstructed images produce image_rejected / model_error, not made-up findings.

What it does

Captures trusted evidence

Uses a webcam on the Pi, validates image quality locally, and stores evidence with reports and traces.

Finds visible H&S risks

Combines Pi-local vision with LAN/Tailscale-hosted reasoning to draft hazards, risk levels and corrective actions.

Shows its working

SQLite memory, Markdown reports, JSON traces, audit-pack export and review controls keep decisions inspectable.

Architecture

1. Camera
Logitech Brio / V4L2 capture on Raspberry Pi 5.
2. Trust gate
Rejects unusable evidence before model analysis.
3. Vision
Ollama moondream describes visible scene evidence.
4. Reasoning
LM Studio over Tailscale drafts H&S findings and actions.
5. Review
Dashboard, reports, CSV/audit export and human approval.

The Vercel deployment is a public project page. The real agent dashboard, camera access, SQLite data and systemd services remain on the Pi.

Built for boring, useful autonomy

No blind claims

If the image is too dark or the model endpoint is unreachable, ProofSight records the failure instead of fabricating findings.

Local-first data

Evidence and reports stay on-device by default. The public site does not expose local inspection data.

Allowlisted actions

The appliance is designed around inspection, reporting and review — not arbitrary shell access from the web.

ProofSight — local AI inspection agent for practical workplace evidence, reports and action plans. Not a replacement for a competent human inspector.
